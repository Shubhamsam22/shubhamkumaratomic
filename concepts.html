Key Concepts from Atomic Habits

  • The 1% Rule: Small improvements each day add up to massive progress. Getting 1% better every day means you're 37 times better by the end of the year.
  • Habit Stacking: Link a new habit to an existing one. For example: “After I brush my teeth, I will write down one thing I’m grateful for.”
  • Identity-Based Habits: Don’t focus just on goals—focus on who you want to become. Instead of saying "I want to run a marathon", say "I am a runner." Your habits shape your identity, and your identity reinforces your habits.
  • The Four Laws of Behavior Change:
    1. Make it obvious
    2. Make it attractive
    3. Make it easy
    4. Make it satisfying
